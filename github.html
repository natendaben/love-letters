GitHub

Where do I start? I’d heard so many things about you, but never got to know you until this year. You made it so easy to get together. I created a cute username for you to recognize me by, and we came up with a password together to make sure everything we do is secure and private.

That being said, you’re not afraid to make our relationship public. I love how other people can view the things we make together, and even contribute to our creations! All I have to do is create a repository — a collection of all the files we are working on together. I can even import an old repository if we don’t want to start from scratch!

You store the history of our relationship so well that I am never nervous about other people messing it up. Besides, if anyone wants to take our relationship in another direction, they can just fork you and make a new repository for themselves. If anyone ever gets jealous and wants to copy our history exactly, they can just clone our work and get an exact replica of our files and past commitments, without it affecting anything between you and me.

Even though it was a little awkward at first that you work with git, who I’ve had a history with, I have to admit that you make it easy to switch between both of you as I please. I can push all of my love and hard work over to you from git, or pull it back again to work on it by myself. You always hold my remote work for me, so that I can come back to you and we can get right back to it. And once I’ve established trust with you, you never need me to prove myself again by logging back in. I can just push and pull whenever I need to. That’s how strong our bond is!

Last, you’re amazing at communication because you’re always have READMEs for me to look over if I get confused. People can markdown whatever they want to tell me about their projects, and I can do the same for them.

I love you so much GitHub! I can’t wait for all of the projects we will build together in the future.

Love,

Nate
