Git

Git,

I know we’ve only been serious for a few weeks now, but it’s never too soon to express my love. I know you always have my back, and for that I can never thank you enough! You keep track of all my changes, and you know my entire history.

To get you going, all I have to do is a simple git init. And you make it so easy to see where we’re at in our relationship! I can git our status whenever I want, to see if you are ready to commit to what we’ve done together. Once I add . my files, I know you’ll constantly track what I do. My friends say it is creepy and clingy, but I know it just proves how dedicated you are to monitoring my changes. Besides, if I ever think it’s too much, I can just put certain things in your .gitignore file, and I know you’ll leave them alone.

I love how we can so easily commit what we’ve made to history, and if I ever want to leave you a message, I can do that too with -m! What’s more, I know you don’t mind me branching out a little bit if I ever want to experiment or take things in a new direction. You know that if you ever get jealous of all my side branches, I can just merge everything to make things simpler between us.

If I ever make a mistake, I know you’ll help me get back to the way things were, no questions asked. All I have to do is checkout to a time when everything was perfect. If I want to, you can help me reflect on how I’ve changed over time with diff. I can truly trust you, Git, more than I have ever trusted past flames like Microsoft Word or Wordpress, which say they are saving my work but have made false promises to me in the past.

One of your best qualities, though, is that I can always look back at our full history together by pulling up our log. It’s so much better than Facebook memories, because --oneline makes things short and sweet, and --since or --until lets me look at specific periods of our time together. So customizable! You only show me what I want to see. You’ve made our history amazing so far, and I know we are going to keep committing to each other for years to come.

XOXO,

Nate
